Data Strategy & Roadmap

Without a clear data strategy and a roadmap to complement it, your data team will fall into the "service trap" -- existing only to solve the crisis of the moment and never driving value.

At one time or another, every data organization falls into the “service trap.” This is when your data organization operates in a wholly reactive state, simply building services to stakeholder specifications or churning out small requests to add metrics, dashboards, or features for end-users.

But, if your users are getting what they need, why is this a problem?

Eventually, this path becomes unsustainable. The gradual fulfillment of feature requests and the creep of small additions eventually lead to the deterioration of your codebase into spaghetti. Counterintuitively, this also leads to user dissatisfaction. It’s not a very positive experience for users to have to constantly ask for every little thing they need and then wait days or, even worse, weeks for it to be implemented.

A well-thought-out roadmap puts the data team back in control. It ensures that solutions are built holistically to allow for future scaling and maintenance. And, when done right, it means the features your users need are available before they ever need to request them.
